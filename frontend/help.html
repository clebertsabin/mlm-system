Mission Requests

The "Mission Requests" feature allows users to request missions for business, travel, or field activities. Hereâ€™s how it works:

  • Enter the Mission Title that describes your mission.
  • Select the Location from a dropdown list. If the location is outside Rwanda, you can enter a custom location.
  • Choose the Date and End Date of your mission.
  • Provide a brief Purpose of the mission.
  • Once you submit, your mission request will be listed under "Previous Mission Requests" with details such as mission title, location, start/end dates, and approval status.

Profile Management

Your profile contains your personal information and allows you to manage various aspects like privacy and contact preferences.

  • Profile Visibility: Set your profile to be visible to everyone, only friends, or set it as private.
  • Contact Preferences: Decide who can contact you: Everyone, Friends, or No One.
  • Notifications: Choose how you would like to receive notifications, either via email or SMS.

Privacy Settings

In this section, you can manage your privacy settings, including who can see your profile and how you can be contacted. You can:

  • Choose to make your profile Public, Private, or visible to Friends Only.
  • Set who can ontact you: Everyone, Friends Only, or No One.
  • Control notifications for events such as mission requests and profile changes.

Dark Mode & Light Mode

The web application supports both Dark Mode and Light Mode for a better user experience. You can toggle between these two themes by clicking the "Toggle Dark/Light Mode" button found in the Settings section.

  • Light Mode: Default theme with light background and dark text.
  • Dark Mode: A theme with a dark background and light text, ideal for low-light environments.

Changing Settings

To update your settings, go to the Settings page where you can change:

  • Your Language preference (English, French, Kinyarwanda).
  • Your Password for account security.
  • Your Notification Preferences, including email and SMS alerts.
